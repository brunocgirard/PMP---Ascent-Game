## Domain Breakdown

### Domain I: People (33% - 14 Tasks)

**Focus**: Skills and activities for effectively leading a project team

**Task List**:
1. **Manage conflict** - Interpret source/stage, evaluate solutions, reconcile conflicts
2. **Lead a team** - Set vision, support diversity, servant leadership, inspire motivation
3. **Support team performance** - Appraise against KPIs, recognize growth, provide feedback
4. **Empower team members** - Organize around strengths, bestow decision-making authority
5. **Ensure adequate training** - Determine competencies, provide training, measure outcomes
6. **Build a team** - Appraise skills, dedicate resources, maintain team composition
7. **Address and remove impediments** - Determine obstacles, prioritize removal, reassess continually
8. **Negotiate project agreements** - Analyze situation, recognize bounds, agree on approach
9. **Collaborate with stakeholders** - Evaluate engagement needs, optimize alignment, build trust
10. **Build shared understanding** - Break down situation, facilitate common mindset, achieve buy-in
11. **Engage and support virtual teams** - Examine needs, investigate alternatives, implement collaboration
12. **Define team ground rules** - Communicate organizational principles, establish team working agreement
13. **Mentor relevant stakeholders** - Allocate time, recognize needs, invest in knowledge transfer
14. **Promote team performance through rewards** - Determine recognition approach, evaluate effectiveness

**Critical Knowledge Areas**:
- Conflict resolution techniques (collaborate, compromise, accommodate, smooth, force, avoid)
- Leadership styles (directive, collaborative, servant, transformational, transactional)
- Team development stages (forming, storming, norming, performing, adjourning)
- Emotional intelligence and interpersonal skills
- Stakeholder engagement strategies
- Virtual team management tools
- Motivation theories (Maslow, Herzberg, McGregor, Expectancy)

---

### Domain II: Process (41% - 17 Tasks)

**Focus**: Technical aspects of managing projects

**Task List**:
1. **Execute project with urgency** - Assess opportunities, integrate new knowledge, allow change
2. **Manage communications** - Analyze needs, determine methods, communicate effectively
3. **Assess and manage risks** - Determine options, iteratively assess risks, recommend responses
4. **Engage stakeholders** - Analyze stakeholders, categorize, engage according to needs
5. **Plan and manage budget and resources** - Estimate costs, anticipate budget challenges
6. **Plan and manage schedule** - Estimate time, determine critical path, monitor variances
7. **Plan and manage quality** - Determine standards, recommend improvements, foster compliance
8. **Plan and manage scope** - Determine requirements, ensure scope alignment, monitor variances
9. **Integrate project planning activities** - Consolidate plans, assess consolidated plan
10. **Manage project changes** - Anticipate changes, execute change strategy, determine response
11. **Plan and manage procurement** - Define resource needs, establish contract strategy
12. **Manage project artifacts** - Determine requirements, validate compliance, prioritize
13. **Determine appropriate project methodology** - Assess needs, recommend project approach
14. **Establish project governance** - Define governance structure, outline reporting requirements
15. **Manage project issues** - Recognize issues, prioritize using urgency/impact
16. **Ensure knowledge transfer** - Articulate information, confirm transfer, share knowledge
17. **Plan and manage project/phase closure** - Determine criteria, validate readiness

**Critical Knowledge Areas**:
- Project management methodologies (Predictive, Agile, Hybrid)
- Schedule management (CPM, PERT, fast tracking, crashing)
- Cost management (EVM, CPI, SPI, EAC, ETC, BAC)
- Risk management (qualitative/quantitative analysis, EMV, decision trees)
- Quality management (Plan-Do-Check-Act, Cost of Quality, 7 QC tools)
- Scope management (WBS, requirements traceability matrix)
- Change control processes
- Procurement types (Fixed price, Cost-reimbursable, T&M)
- Agile ceremonies (Sprint Planning, Daily Standup, Review, Retrospective)
- Agile artifacts (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment)

---

### Domain III: Business Environment (26% - 4 Tasks)

**Focus**: Connection between projects and organizational strategy

**Task List**:
1. **Plan and manage project compliance** - Confirm requirements, classify compliance categories, determine impact
2. **Evaluate and deliver project benefits and value** - Investigate value, appraise stakeholder value, advise on realization
3. **Evaluate and address external business environment changes** - Survey external environment, assess impacts, recommend responses
4. **Support organizational change** - Assess readiness, formulate approach, integrate into plan

**Critical Knowledge Areas**:
- Organizational structures (Functional, Matrix, Projectized)
- Benefits realization and value delivery
- Business case development and ROI analysis
- Regulatory compliance and standards
- Environmental, social, governance (ESG) factors
- Change management frameworks (ADKAR, Kotter's 8-Step)
- Stakeholder change adoption strategies
- Portfolio, program, and project relationships
- Strategic alignment and business value

## Learning Requirements by Domain

### Required Learning Components

Each domain task requires learners to master:

1. **Conceptual Knowledge** (KNOW)
   - Definitions, frameworks, and theoretical foundations
   - PMI terminology and standard practices
   - Industry best practices

2. **Application Skills** (APPLY)
   - Scenario-based application of concepts
   - Decision-making in context
   - Tool and technique selection

3. **Evaluation & Analysis** (ANALYZE)
   - Critical thinking and problem-solving
   - Trade-off analysis
   - Situational judgment

### Learning Progression Model

```
Foundation ‚Üí Application ‚Üí Mastery ‚Üí Certification Readiness

Level 1: Foundation (0-25%)
- Read and understand core concepts
- Memorize key terms and definitions
- Recognize basic patterns

Level 2: Application (25-50%)
- Apply concepts to simple scenarios
- Differentiate between techniques
- Make basic decisions

Level 3: Integration (50-75%)
- Integrate multiple knowledge areas
- Analyze complex scenarios
- Evaluate trade-offs

Level 4: Mastery (75-90%)
- Synthesize across domains
- Make expert judgments
- Predict outcomes

Level 5: Certification Ready (90-100%)
- Consistently score 75%+ on practice exams
- Complete timed exams within limits
- Demonstrate confidence across all domains
```

## Knowledge Assessment Requirements

### Assessment Types Needed

1. **Flashcard Review** (Spaced Repetition)
   - Purpose: Memorization of terms, concepts, formulas
   - Method: Leitner 5-box system
   - Target: 460+ flashcards covering all domains
   - Success: All cards in Box 4-5 (mastered)

2. **Topic Quizzes** (Knowledge Check)
   - Purpose: Verify understanding of individual tasks
   - Format: 10-15 questions per task
   - Target: 75%+ accuracy per topic
   - Success: 90%+ topics scoring above threshold

3. **Domain Drills** (Focused Practice)
   - Purpose: Build domain-specific expertise
   - Format: 20-30 questions per domain
   - Target: Unlimited practice until mastery
   - Success: Consistent 80%+ scores

4. **Mixed Practice** (Cross-Domain Integration)
   - Purpose: Integrate knowledge across domains
   - Format: 50-75 questions, mixed domains
   - Target: Simulate realistic exam mix
   - Success: 75%+ accuracy

5. **Mock Exams** (Certification Readiness)
   - Purpose: Full exam simulation
   - Format: 180 questions, 240 minutes, real distribution
   - Target: At least 3 full mock exams
   - Success: 2 consecutive scores of 75%+

### Progressive Difficulty Levels

| Level | Difficulty | Question Style | Purpose |
|-------|-----------|----------------|---------|
| **1. Recall** | Easy | Direct fact recall | Build foundation |
| **2. Application** | Medium | Simple scenarios | Apply concepts |
| **3. Analysis** | Hard | Complex scenarios | Critical thinking |
| **4. Synthesis** | Very Hard | Multi-domain integration | Exam-level mastery |
